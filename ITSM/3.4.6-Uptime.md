An SLA of **99.999% uptime** (often called “five nines”) means only **0.001% downtime** is allowed.

Let’s translate that into actual time:

***

## 📅 Per Year

*   Total minutes in a year: **525,600**
*   Allowed downtime:
        525,600 × 0.00001 = 5.256 minutes

✅ **≈ 5 minutes 15 seconds per year**

***

## 📅 Per Month (30 days)

*   Total minutes: **43,200**
*   Allowed downtime:
        43,200 × 0.00001 = 0.432 minutes

✅ **≈ 26 seconds per month**

***

## 📅 Per Day

*   Total minutes: **1,440**
*   Allowed downtime:
        1,440 × 0.00001 = 0.0144 minutes

✅ **≈ 0.86 seconds per day**

***

## ⏱️ Quick Summary

| Time Period | Max Downtime   |
| ----------- | -------------- |
| Year        | \~5 min 15 sec |
| Month       | \~26 seconds   |
| Day         | < 1 second     |

***

## 🧠 Takeaway

At **99.999% uptime**, systems must be extremely reliable—  
even a **short outage of a few minutes per year** can violate the SLA.

***

Here’s a clear comparison between **99.9% (three nines)** and **99.999% (five nines)** uptime:

***

# 📊 Uptime Comparison

## ✅ Downtime Allowance

| Uptime SLA  | Downtime per Year | Downtime per Month | Downtime per Day |
| ----------- | ----------------- | ------------------ | ---------------- |
| **99.9%**   | \~8 hours 46 min  | \~43 minutes       | \~1 min 26 sec   |
| **99.999%** | \~5 min 15 sec    | \~26 seconds       | < 1 second       |

***

## 🔍 What This Means

### 🟡 99.9% (Three Nines)

*   Allows **significant downtime**
*   Outages can last **tens of minutes per month**
*   Usually acceptable for:
    *   Internal business apps
    *   Non-critical services
    *   Small websites

👉 Example: A server could go down for nearly **45 minutes in a month** and still meet SLA.

***

### 🟢 99.999% (Five Nines)

*   Requires **extreme reliability**
*   Downtime is measured in **seconds, not minutes**
*   Used for:
    *   Financial systems
    *   Healthcare systems
    *   Telecom and cloud infrastructure

👉 Example: Even **a few minutes of downtime per year** breaks the SLA.

***

## ⚖️ Key Difference

| Factor                | 99.9%               | 99.999%                    |
| --------------------- | ------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Tolerance for failure | Moderate            | Extremely low              |
| Engineering effort    | Standard redundancy | Advanced high availability |
| Cost                  | Lower               | Much higher                |
| Complexity            | Moderate            | Very high                  |

***

## 🧠 Big Insight

The difference looks small (**99.9 → 99.999**), but:

*   Downtime drops from **\~9 hours/year → \~5 minutes/year**
*   That’s about **100× less downtime**

***

## 🚀 Takeaway

*   **99.9%** = “mostly reliable”
*   **99.999%** = “mission-critical, nearly always on”
